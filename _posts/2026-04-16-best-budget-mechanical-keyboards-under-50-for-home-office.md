# What to Look For

When shopping for a budget mechanical keyboard, there are several key factors to consider. Firstly, look for a keyboard with a durable construction and a compact tenkeyless design, which can help reduce costs without sacrificing performance. Secondly, consider the type of switches used, such as Cherry MX or Kailh, which can affect the typing experience and overall quality of the keyboard. Thirdly, check the keyboard's connectivity options, such as USB or wireless, and ensure it is compatible with your computer or laptop. Lastly, think about the keyboard's features, such as backlighting, macro keys, or media controls, and whether they are essential for your work or gaming needs.
